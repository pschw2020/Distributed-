1. Traditional Central System Limitations

↑ Top

Single-Thermostat Operation

Traditional residential HVAC systems operate with:

  • Single thermostat control for entire building
  • Full system capacity required at all times
  • All zones conditioned simultaneously regardless of occupancy
  • Peak design capacity sized for worst-case conditions

Example: Typical 2,500 sq ft residence

  • Central system sizing: 4 tons cooling capacity
  • Operates at full capacity when any zone calls
  • Unused rooms receive conditioned air
  • Duct losses: 15-25% of delivered air

2. Distributed Hydronic Fan Coil Advantages

↑ Top

Zone-Level Control Architecture

Distributed hydronic fan coil systems replace a single central ducted unit with:

  • Multiple compact fan coils, each serving a separate zone
  • Individual thermostats in each zone
  • Hydronic distribution via insulated water lines
  • On-demand conditioning only where needed

Zoned conditioning reduces peak system requirements due to diversity of load and occupancy patterns.

Energy Savings Potential

Research findings (ScienceDirect, 2022):

  • Zoned hydronic systems achieve up to 40% energy savings in residential dwellings
  • High thermal comfort maintained in all zones
  • Payback periods under 4.9 years demonstrated

Energy Reduction Mechanisms

  1. Only occupied zones are conditioned
  2. No thermal losses through ductwork
  3. Improved part-load efficiency
  4. Reduced cycling losses through continuous modulation

3. ThermoLift Integration: COP and AFUE Optimization

↑ Top

Heating Mode Performance

ThermoLift performance is maximized when delivering low-temperature water:

  • Optimal supply water temp: 110°F
  • Lower discharge temperatures yield higher COP
  • Distributed fan coils can operate effectively at 110°F with proper sizing

Cooling Mode Performance

Chilled water operation is standard for fan coil systems:

  • Typical chilled water temp: 45°F
  • Fully compatible with hydronic fan coil units
  • Allows ThermoLift to operate in cooling mode effectively

AFUE Considerations

ThermoLift offers high AFUE (up to 150%+) compared to conventional boilers:

  • Distributed zoning reduces runtime and gas consumption
  • Maintains higher efficiency by operating closer to ideal part-load conditions
  • Eliminates duct losses present in forced-air systems

5. System Design Implications for ThermoLift

↑ Top

Capacity Sizing Approach

Design should account for:

  • Reduced total capacity due to zoning diversity
  • Proper fan coil selection per zone load profile
  • Maintaining low supply temps for COP gains

Hydraulic Design Considerations

  • Insulated PEX lines to each zone
  • Balancing valves and flow control
  • Condensate drainage integrated per unit
  • Appropriate pumping and pressure drop management
